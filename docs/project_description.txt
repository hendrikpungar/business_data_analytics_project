Project Description
For this project, you will work with a large credit card customer transaction dataset of approximately 100,000 records and about 1000 clients. The main goal is to transform raw financial and transactional data into useful business insights that can support decision-making in a banking or financial services context.

The project should not focus only on building machine learning models. The emphasis should be on understanding customers, identifying business opportunities, detecting risk, and making practical recommendations for customer management, marketing, and profitability.

you  will use the dataset to analyse customer behaviour, segment customers, predict customer value, estimate churn or inactivity risk, and produce a short forecast of future spending or balance behaviour.

Inroduction
Banks and financial institutions need to understand how customers use their credit cards, which customers are most valuable, which customers may stop using the product, and which customer groups require different marketing or risk strategies.

Using the available customer, account, and transaction data, students will develop a business analytics project that answers questions such as:

Which customers are the most valuable?

Which customers are at risk of churn or inactivity?

What types of customers spend more?

How can customers be grouped into meaningful business segments?

Can we estimate future customer value?

Can we forecast future spending trends?

What actions should the business take based on the analysis?

Main Objectives
The project should address four main business analytics areas:

1. Customer Segmentation
Students should group customers into meaningful segments based on their financial and behavioural characteristics.

Possible segmentation variables include:

Credit limit
Month-end balance
Transaction amount
Monthly payment
Age
Card type
Total number of cards
Merchant groups
Transaction frequency
Average transaction amount
Credit utilisation ratio
Possible customer segments:

Segment	Description
High-value customers	High spending, high credit limit, regular payments
Low-activity customers	Few transactions, low spending
Revolvers	High month-end balance, low payment ratio
Transactors	Frequent spending, balance paid regularly
Risk-sensitive customers	High utilisation, irregular payments
Category-focused customers	Strong spending in petrol, grocery, retail, or travel
The purpose of segmentation is to help the business design better strategies for different types of customers.

Example business actions:

Reward high-value customers
Re-engage low-activity customers
Offer instalment products to heavy spenders
Monitor high-utilisation customers
Create merchant-specific promotions
2. Regression: Predicting Customer Value or Financial Behaviour
Build a regression model to predict a continuous business outcome.

Recommended regression target:

Customer Lifetime Value estimate
Estimated the CLTV variable.

Estimated CLTV = total customer spending × assumed revenue rate
Regression models may include:

Linear Regression
Random Forest Regressor
Gradient Boosting Regressor
Business use:

The bank can estimate which customers are likely to generate higher value and prioritise them for loyalty programmes, personalised offers, or premium card upgrades.

3. Classification: Predicting Churn or Inactivity Risk
Students should create a classification model to identify customers who may be at risk of churn, inactivity, or account problems.

Since the dataset may not have a direct CHURN column, students can create a business-defined churn proxy.

Possible churn definitions:

Customer has very low or no recent transaction activity
Customer has inactive or blocked card status
Customer has no spending in the latest observed period
Customer shows a sharp decline in spending compared with previous months
Example binary target:

Churn_Risk = 1 if customer has no transactions in the most recent month
Churn_Risk = 0 otherwise
Classification models may include:

Logistic Regression
Random Forest
Important classification metrics:

Accuracy
Precision
Recall
F1-score
ROC-AUC
Confusion Matrix
For churn, recall is especially important because the business wants to identify as many at-risk customers as possible before they leave.

Business use:

The bank can use churn prediction to launch retention campaigns, offer rewards, reduce inactivity, and protect future revenue.

4. Forecasting: Short-Term Spending or Balance Trends
include a small forecasting component to estimate future customer or portfolio behaviour.

Forecast Monthly Total Spending
Aggregate transactions by month:

Month → Total AMOUNT
They can also forecast by merchant group:

Month + Merchant Group → Total Spending
Possible forecasting methods:

Moving average
ARIMA
Regression with lag variables
Business use:

Forecasting helps the bank anticipate future card usage, plan marketing campaigns, estimate transaction revenue, and identify seasonal changes in customer spending.

create business-relevant variables, not only technical ones.

Account age

Total customer spending

Number of merchant groups used

These features should help explain customer value, risk, churn, and behaviour.
